Medication Management

We review and organise prescriptions, set up weekly/biweekly pillboxes with clear labelling, coordinate refills (with signed authorisation) and send simple updates after each visit.

  • Medication list reconciliation
  • Pillbox filling and labelling
  • Refill coordination & med synchronisation

DME Cleaning

We clean and sanitise essential durable medical equipment—CPAP machines, oxygen concentrators, nebulisers, walkers, wheelchairs and canes—following manufacturer guidance to support safety and comfort.

  • Sanitisation of CPAP, concentrators & nebulisers
  • Cleaning of walkers, wheelchairs & canes
  • Inspection and maintenance reminders

Monthly DME Sanitisation

We clean and disinfect durable medical equipment—including CPAP masks and tubing, walkers, nebulisers, commodes and more—using manufacturer‑recommended techniques to ensure safety and longevity.

  • CPAP and oxygen equipment
  • Walkers, canes, rollators & wheelchairs
  • Nebulisers and commodes

Family & Caregiver Updates

Transparency builds trust. After each visit, we send concise notes and photos to keep families informed. You’ll know exactly what was done and when.

  • Summary of tasks performed
  • Photos of organised medications or sanitised equipment
  • Secure digital log accessible to authorised family members

Our process, step by step

  • Intake & Onboarding: We gather health history, current medications and prescriber information. A clinician visits to meet you and set up your plan.
  • Weekly Pillbox Prep: We reconcile prescriptions, fill pillboxes and label doses. Any discrepancies are checked with your pharmacist or doctor.
  • Daily Reminders: Custom call or text reminders ensure doses aren’t missed. We follow up quickly if a dose is delayed.
  • Monthly Sanitisation: We clean CPAP masks, walkers and nebulisers using manufacturer guidelines to maximise safety and lifespan.
  • Reporting & Updates: After each visit you receive notes and photos. We coordinate with your care team and adjust plans as needed.
